My work expeirence so far

I started my web development journey during the COVID-19 pandemic lockdown. It was a time in my life when I was forced to stay home and really think about what I wanted to do in life. Seeing as how every non-essential business was forced to close to prevent the spread of a highly contagious virus. The idea of programming came from a close uncle who has become the main mentorship figure in my life. He explained how he had recently picked up programming as a hobby. He recommended I start out with Python 3 as an introductory language. This seemed like the perfect opportunity to do so. I started with courses on Codecademy, which he had recommended as a good platform to learn and get certified, as Codecademy is used by many big tech companies such as NASA, IBM, and many more.

When I first started coding I set goals for myself using the Codecademy platform. I aimed to study Python 3, 4 hours a day, 5 days a week. I kept myself accountable and was able to achieve a 7-week long streak without missing a single day. I was motivated by a saying that was taught to me in the military. "What you put in is what you get out.". At the time this was in reference to the effort I should place into our physical training exercise. This saying really resonated with me because it was just such a true and simple statement. This really is what everything boils down to. This really pushes me to stay honest with myself and put in the best and most effort I could into things so that when I face challenges, I am ready to overcome them. There were many insistences where I was stuck on a concept or project during my courses. I felt the need to want to give up and try another day. However, I know this would not give me the results I needed. If I wanted to achieve the goal of being a professional programmer I would have to put in a great amount of effort and keep using every resource I can to better understand different concepts or finish projects.

After I learned Python 3. I started to think about my next steps. During my journey, I learned about the different roles and functions of computer languages. I learned about the different roles front-end, back end and full-stack developers play. I felt front-end development had caught my attention the most. One thing I liked about the front end was seeing your code come to life in the workspace. Writing the content with HTML and designing and styling it with CSS seemed very enjoyable. I started the learn HTML 5 course on Codecademy and kept a steady pace of studying 5 days a week. I learned many new rules of HTML and syntax. Afterward, I continued on the path of front-end programming with CSS and lastly Javascript.
